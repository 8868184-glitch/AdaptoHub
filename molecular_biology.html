üìñ Introduction to Molecular Biology

Molecular biology studies the molecular basis of biological activity, focusing on the structure and function of macromolecules essential to life, particularly DNA, RNA, and proteins.

What You'll Learn:

‚úì DNA replication
‚úì Transcription (DNA to RNA)

üéØ The Central Dogma

The flow of genetic information in cells:

DNA ‚Üí RNA ‚Üí Protein

‚Ä¢ Replication: DNA ‚Üí DNA (copying genetic material)
‚Ä¢ Transcription: DNA ‚Üí RNA (making RNA from DNA)
‚Ä¢ Translation: RNA ‚Üí Protein (making proteins from RNA)

‚ö° DNA Replication

The process of copying DNA before cell division to pass genetic information to daughter cells.

Replication Steps:

1. Unwinding: Helicase unzips the double helix
2. Primer binding: Primase adds RNA primers
3. Elongation: DNA polymerase adds complementary nucleotides
4. Proofreading: DNA polymerase checks for errors
5. Joining: Ligase seals gaps between fragments

‚Ä¢ Semi-conservative: Each new DNA molecule has one old strand, one new strand
‚Ä¢ Occurs in S phase of cell cycle
‚Ä¢ Extremely accurate (~1 error per billion bases)

üî¨ Transcription

The synthesis of RNA from a DNA template. Creates messenger RNA (mRNA) that carries genetic instructions.

Transcription Process:

1. Initiation: RNA polymerase binds to promoter region
2. Elongation: RNA polymerase reads DNA, synthesizes RNA
3. Termination: Reaches terminator sequence, releases RNA

DNA to RNA Base Pairing

DNA strand: A-T-G-C-G-A
RNA produced: U-A-C-G-C-U
(Note: RNA has Uracil (U) instead of Thymine (T))

RNA Processing (Eukaryotes)

‚Ä¢ 5' cap: Protective cap added to start
‚Ä¢ Poly-A tail: Added to end for stability
‚Ä¢ Splicing: Introns (non-coding) removed, exons (coding) joined

üß™ Translation

The synthesis of proteins from mRNA instructions. Occurs on ribosomes.

Translation Steps:

1. Initiation: mRNA binds to ribosome, start codon (AUG) recognized
2. Elongation: tRNA brings amino acids, ribosome links them
3. Termination: Stop codon reached, protein released

The Genetic Code

‚Ä¢ Triplet code: 3 nucleotides (codon) = 1 amino acid
‚Ä¢ 64 possible codons code for 20 amino acids
‚Ä¢ Universal: Same in nearly all organisms
‚Ä¢ Start codon: AUG (Methionine)
‚Ä¢ Stop codons: UAA, UAG, UGA

Translation Example

mRNA: AUG-UUU-GGC-UAA
Amino acids: Met-Phe-Gly-STOP
Protein: Met-Phe-Gly (3 amino acids long)

üìä Gene Expression and Regulation

Cells control which genes are expressed and when, allowing specialization.

Regulation Mechanisms:

‚Ä¢ Transcriptional control: Turning genes on/off
‚Ä¢ Epigenetic modifications: DNA methylation, histone modification
‚Ä¢ Post-transcriptional: mRNA processing, stability
‚Ä¢ Translational control: Controlling protein synthesis
‚Ä¢ Post-translational: Protein modifications

Example: Lac Operon (Bacteria)

Genes for lactose metabolism only expressed when lactose is present and glucose is absent - efficient resource use.
